## ✅ Why LIGHTER Indigo (Not Darker) Is Better

### **Readability & Contrast**

```
DARK INDIGO (#1a1f3a):
├─ White text: LOW contrast (hard to read)
├─ Light gray text: VERY LOW contrast
├─ On dark backgrounds: Completely invisible
└─ Verdict: TOO DARK

MEDIUM INDIGO (#3d4f8f - RECOMMENDED):
├─ White text: HIGH contrast (easy to read)
├─ Light gray text: MEDIUM contrast
├─ On dark backgrounds: Visible
└─ Verdict: PERFECT BALANCE

WCAG Accessibility:
├─ Dark Indigo: Fails some contrast tests
├─ Medium Indigo: Passes all contrast tests
└─ Legal requirement for accessible SaaS
```

### **Color Psychology Impact**

```
DARK INDIGO feels:
├─ Heavy (feels tiring after 8-hour workday)
├─ Corporate (feels stuffy, old)
├─ Technical (good) but intimidating (bad)

MEDIUM INDIGO feels:
├─ Confident (not heavy)
├─ Premium (feels like high-end software)
├─ Technical AND approachable
└─ Professional AND modern
```

// tailwind.config.js
