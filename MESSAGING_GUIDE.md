### FOR LEGAL/ENGINEERING FIRMS (Not the primary buyer; positioning)

**Headline:** "RegGuard accelerates your intake. Your firm stays in control of final filings."

**Solution:** "Position RegGuard to your clients as a *preliminary research tool* that you review and refine before filing. Faster turnaround for clients. Cheaper fees for them. Your firm's final review is the bottleneck and value."

**Why RegGuard:**
- **You stay in control:** Every RegGuard worksheet goes through your attorney/engineer review before filing
- **Cheaper for clients:** Clients pay less overall ($15K RegGuard + $50K your review vs. $150K your full engagement)
- **Faster for clients:** You get preliminary work done for them; you focus on final review + negotiation (higher-value work)

**Positioning:** "We're not replacing you. We're the research layer. You do the expert review and final filings."

## Key Phrases to Use (Repeat)

1. **"60–80% cheaper than law firms"** — Always anchor to counsel cost, not SaaS competition
2. **"48 hours, not 6 weeks"** — Speed is the differentiator
3. **"Cited sources for audit trails"** — Builds trust with lenders/investors
4. **"Accelerates intake; your counsel reviews before filing"** — Clarifies our role (not replacing counsel)
5. **"Kill bad sites early"** — ROI angle (prevents $2M–$5M waste)
6. **"One report pays for a year's Enterprise license"** — Volume justification

---

## Phrases to Avoid

1. ❌ **"AI compliance platform"** — Sounds like boring SaaS (e.g., Procore)
2. ❌ **"Unlimited reports for $60K/year"** — Devalues the product (sounds like freemium)
3. ❌ **"Advanced NLP + semantic search"** — Buyers don't care about tech; they care about outcomes
4. ❌ **"Replaces interconnection consultants"** — Creates friction with our partner channel
5. ❌ **"No legal advice needed"** — Legally risky messaging; we always recommend counsel review
6. ❌ **"AI-powered due diligence"** — Too trendy; doesn't differentiate

## Sales Conversation Flow

### Step 1: Problem Discovery
**"When you screen a new data center site, how long does it take to get preliminary interconnection research from your counsel? And what does that cost?"**

*Expected answer: 2–4 weeks, $75K–$150K*

### Step 2: Opportunity Framing
**"What if you could get that same research—with cited sources—in 48 hours for $15K? How would that change your site screening process?"**

### Step 3: Proof
**"Let me show you what a RegGuard report looks like."** *(demo sample report)*

### Step 4: Qualification
**"How many sites do you typically screen per year? If you screen 5+, $60K/year Enterprise plan pays for itself vs. counsel."**

### Step 5: Call to Action
**"Let's pilot on your next 2–3 sites. You'll see the time/cost savings immediately. Then we can talk annual contract."**

## Pricing Communication

### Hybrid Plan ($15K + $20K/year)
**Best for:** Developers who screen 1–3 sites/year

"Pay per report ($15K) for single sites. If you commit to annual monitoring, $20K/year includes 2–3 additional reports + quarterly regulatory updates."

### Enterprise Plan ($60K/year)
**Best for:** PE firms, consultants, developers screening 5+ sites/year

"Unlimited reports. At 5+ sites/year, your cost drops to $12K/site. Includes priority 24-hour turnaround, quarterly strategy calls, and API access."

### Why Hybrid + Enterprise (not subscription-only)

"We learned that one-time project pricing ($15K per report) is what makes sense for developers. But if you're continuously screening sites, Enterprise ($60K/year unlimited) is cheaper on a per-site basis. Pick the model that matches your volume."
